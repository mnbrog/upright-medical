Predict Risk. Prevent Falls.

Our Fall Risk Assessment (FRA) technology allows clinicians to quickly identify critical areas of fall risk. This detailed, automated assessment calculates an individual’s statistical risk of falling within the next 12 months, generating individualized recommendations for care.

  • Automatically calculates advanced mathematics needed to predict fall risk.
  • Leverages data from over 2,300 separate studies.
  • Complete the test in just 10–25 minutes.
  • Rooted in research, yet simple to conduct.

How It Works

  1. Patient Screening – Patients self‑answer questionnaires in waiting room or at home.
  2. Performance Testing – MA administers 3–5 tests (Five Time Sit to Stand, Self Paced Walking Test, Single Leg Stance, Timed Up and Go).
  3. Review Report & Care Plan – Doctor reviews auto‑generated FRA report and care plan.

Performance Tests

  • Five Time Sit to Stand: Measures lower‑limb strength and balance.
  • Self Paced Walking Test: Assesses gait speed and stability.
  • Single Leg Stance: Tests static balance on one limb.
  • Timed Up and Go: Evaluates dynamic balance and mobility.

Performance‑Based Outcome Measures

Performance‑Based Outcome Measures (PBOMs) are the most powerful predictors of future falls. Lusardi et al. (2017) combined multiple PBOMs for improved accuracy. Parcetich et al. (2022) compared Lusardi’s PBOMs (APTA‑SR/3) versus CDC STEADI and found higher positive likelihood ratios for APTA‑SR/3. Nithman et al. (2019) showed STEADI can have high false negative rates—hence, it’s important to conduct more than one mobility or balance test.

Billing Information

Average procedure reimbursement: $73.48 (range $45–$136). Clients use code CPT 97750 (Physical Performance Test) billed in 15‑minute units. Rounding rules: 8 minutes = 1 unit, 23 minutes = 2 units, 38 minutes = 3 units. For self‑report questionnaire interpretation, use CPT 96160—two questionnaires may be used in the protocol.

Plan of Care & Educational Materials

Once a patient’s FRA report is complete, the doctor reviews results with the patient, provides a Falls Care Plan, and offers educational materials (e.g., brochures on home modifications, exercise tips).
